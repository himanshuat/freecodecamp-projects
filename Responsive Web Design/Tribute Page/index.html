Sachin Tendulkar

Sachin Tendulkar being carried by his teammates after winning the ICC World Cup 2011 Final between India and Sri Lanka at the Wankhede Stadium on April 2, 2011 in Mumbai, India

Sachin Ramesh Tendulkar is a former Indian cricketer who was born on 24th of April, 1973 in Bombay, Maharashtra. He is regarded as one of the world's best batsmen. He is a part time bowler which can bowl both off and leg breaks.

Highlights of Sachin's Career

  • Sachin was trained under Ramakant Achrekar during his school days. He shifted schools to be a part of the best teams in the city.
  • He made his International debut at the age of 16 in the year 1989 against arch rivals Pakistan, scoring 15 runs before being bowled by Waqar Younis.
  • In his ODI debut, he was dismissed for a duck by Waqar against Pakistan in Gujranwala in 1989
  • In 1990, he scored a test century against England in Old Trafford, Manchester saving his team from a defeat and becoming the second youngest to score a test century.
  • In 2001, Tendulkar became the first player to score 10,000 runs in One Day International (ODI) competition, and the following year he surpassed the great Don Bradman on the all-time list with his 30th Test century
  • Tendulkar made 673 runs in 11 matches in the 2003 Cricket World Cup, helping India reach the final. While Australia retained the trophy that they had won in 1999, Tendulkar was given the Man of the Tournament award
  • In October 2008, he entered the record books again by blowing past Brian Lara's mark of 11,953 Test runs. On the heels of becoming the first player to post a double century in ODI play, he was named the 2010 International Cricket Council Cricketer of the Year
  • In April 2011, Tendulkar chalked up another milestone when he and his team propelled India to a World Cup victory over Sri Lanka, the first in his long career. During the tournament, he become the first batsman to score 2,000 runs and six centuries in Cricket World Cup
  • Tendulkar announced his retirement from One Day Internationals on 23 December 2012
  • On 10 October 2013 Tendulkar announced that he would retire from all cricket after the two-Test series against West Indies in November
  • Tendulkar received the Arjuna Award in 1994 for his outstanding sporting achievement, the Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna award in 1997, India's highest sporting honour, and the Padma Shri and Padma Vibhushan awards in 1999 and 2008, respectively, India's fourth- and second-highest civilian awards
  • After a few hours of his final match on 16 November 2013, the Prime Minister's Office announced the decision to award him the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award. He is the youngest recipient to date and the first ever sportsperson to receive the award

Some sensational records of Sachin

  • He amassed 15,921 and 18,426 runs in Tests and ODIs respectively and is the only player to score more than 30,000 runs in international cricket
  • He is the 16th player and the first Indian to score 50,000 runs in all forms of domestic and international recognised cricket (First-class, List A and Twenty20)
  • He holds the record for the highest number of centuries in international cricket, with a tally of 100{Tests (51) and ODIs (49)}
  • He has scored over 1,000 runs in a calendar year in ODIs 7 times, and in 1998 he scored 1,894 runs, the record for the highest number of runs scored by any player in a single calendar year for One-Day Internationals
  • He is the first batsman to score a double century in ODI history
